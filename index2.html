Aliko Dangote

Life Story

Aliko Dangote, born on April 10, 1957, in Kano, Nigeria, is a Nigerian business magnate and philanthropist. He was born into a wealthy Muslim family of traders and started showing an interest in business at a young age. Dangote completed his primary and secondary education in Nigeria and later attended the Al-Azhar University in Cairo, Egypt.

After completing his studies, Dangote returned to Nigeria and started his first business venture in 1977. He established a small trading firm, which dealt with the import and distribution of basic household goods, including sugar, rice, and cement. Over time, his business expanded, and he ventured into various industries.

Businesses

  • Dangote Group: A multinational industrial conglomerate involved in various sectors, including cement production, sugar refining, flour milling, salt processing, and more.
  • Dangote Cement: One of the largest cement manufacturers in Africa, with operations in several countries.
  • Dangote Sugar Refinery: Nigeria's largest sugar refinery, producing refined sugar for domestic and industrial use.
  • Dangote Flour Mills: A leading flour milling company in Nigeria, producing a wide range of flour-based products.
  • Dangote Refinery and Petrochemical: Currently under construction, the refinery is expected to be one of the world's largest oil refineries when completed.
  • Dangote Fertilizer: A major player in the fertilizer industry, aimed at boosting agricultural productivity in Nigeria and Africa.

Philanthropy

Aliko Dangote is also known for his philanthropic activities. He has made significant contributions to education, healthcare, and poverty alleviation in Nigeria and across Africa. Through the Dangote Foundation, he has funded numerous projects, including the construction of schools, hospitals, and scholarships for students.
